Short About Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi (1869–1948) was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who led India’s non-violent independence movement against British rule. Known for his philosophy of Satyagraha (truth and non-violence), Gandhi employed civil disobedience, peaceful protests, and boycotts to challenge British authority. His leadership during pivotal moments, such as the Salt March (1930) and Quit India Movement (1942), mobilized millions in India.
Gandhi's principles of non-violence influenced civil rights movements worldwide, including leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela. Tragically, he was assassinated in 1948 by a Hindu nationalist who opposed his vision of religious tolerance and unity.

Nelson Mandela (1918–2013) was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who became the country's first Black president from 1994 to 1999. Imprisoned for 27 years for his activism against the racist apartheid regime, Mandela is renowned for his commitment to non-violence, reconciliation, and equality. His leadership in dismantling apartheid and fostering a peaceful transition to democracy made him a global symbol of resilience, justice, and forgiveness. Mandela’s legacy continues to inspire movements for human rights and social justice worldwide.

Short about Adolf Hitler

Adolf Hitler (1889–1945) was a German politician and leader of the National Socialist German Workers' Party (Nazi Party). He became Chancellor of Germany in 1933 and later Führer in 1934, establishing a totalitarian regime characterized by extreme nationalism, militarism, and racism. Hitler is infamous for initiating World War II by invading Poland in 1939 and for orchestrating the Holocaust, which led to the systematic genocide of six million Jews and millions of others deemed "undesirable." His aggressive expansionist policies and totalitarian rule resulted in widespread destruction and loss of life. Hitler died by suicide in 1945 as Allied forces closed in on Berlin, marking the end of the Nazi regime. His legacy is a stark reminder of the dangers of totalitarianism, hatred, and intolerance.

Short About Anuradha Koirala

Anuradha Koirala is a renowned Nepalese social activist and the founder of Maiti Nepal, an organization dedicated to combating human trafficking, rescuing, and rehabilitating survivors. Known as the "Mother Teresa of Nepal," she has devoted her life to protecting and empowering vulnerable women and children. Her work has garnered international recognition, including awards like CNN Hero of the Year in 2010. Through her compassionate efforts, Koirala has helped thousands reclaim their lives and has inspired global action against human trafficking.
